Question and Answer

1. Why is it important to fertilize your lawn?

Fertilizing your lawn is important because it provides the necessary nutrients to keep it healthy and green. A well-fertilized yard can also help prevent weeds and pests from taking up residence.

2. How often should I mow my lawn?

The frequency of mowing your lawn will depend on the type of grass you have and the current weather conditions. As a general rule, you should aim to mow your lawn at least once a week.

3. What steps do I need to take to prepare my lawn for winter?

To prepare your lawn for winter, you should fertilize it with a slow-release fertilizer, remove any leaves or debris, and continue to mow the grass until it stops growing. You may also want to consider aerating your lawn to help reduce soil compaction.

4. How long does it take to see results from lawn fertilization?

The length of time it takes to see results from lawn fertilization will depend on the type of fertilizer used and the condition of your lawn. In most cases, you can expect to see results within four to six weeks.
